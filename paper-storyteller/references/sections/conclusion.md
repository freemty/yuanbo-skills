# Conclusion Guide

## Narrative Goal

Return to the intro's opening image. Complete the loop. Then zoom out — what does this work mean for a bigger question?

## Writing Rhythm

```
¶1  Revisit core insight — NOT a repeat of the abstract, but a mature restatement now that the reader has seen the evidence
¶2  Strongest evidence — the most compelling experimental results, briefly
¶3  Limitations — honest, specific, tied to scope (not fixable implementation flaws)
¶4  Future + Zoom-out — point toward a bigger question, then stop
```

## The Closing Loop

The conclusion's first sentence should echo (not copy) the intro's hook.

- If intro opened with a question → conclusion answers it with confidence earned from experiments
- If intro opened with a scene → conclusion returns to the scene, transformed by the new understanding
- If intro opened with an observation → conclusion reframes the observation in light of the contribution

## Limitations

Prefer scope limitations over implementation flaws:
- Data regime ("currently limited to short sequences")
- Assumption boundary ("requires controlled viewpoints")
- Deployment scope ("tested on specific sensor setup")

## The Final Sentences (Freeman-style Negative Space)

The last 1-2 sentences point toward the future but don't spell it out. Leave room for the reader's imagination.

**Do:** "These findings suggest that [broader principle], opening new questions about [bigger territory]."
**Don't:** "In future work, we plan to extend our method to [specific next step 1], [specific next step 2], and [specific next step 3]."

The reader should close the paper thinking, not checking off a to-do list.

## Quality Checklist

1. Does the opening echo the intro's hook?
2. Is the core insight restated (not just the abstract repeated)?
3. Are limitations honest and scope-based?
4. Do the final sentences zoom out and leave space?
