# Related Work Guide

## Narrative Goal

Draw a map of the intellectual landscape, then point to your location on it. This is NOT a literature review — it's positioning.

## Writing Rhythm

Organize by **intellectual thread**, not by chronology or method category.

```
Thread 1: [Foundational approach] — who started it, key evolution, where it hits its limit
Thread 2: [Alternative approach] — different angle, strengths, but still misses [X]
Thread 3: [Your lineage] — the thread your work extends, and what you add
```

Each thread ends by noting the relationship to your work.

## Paragraph Template

1. **Topic sentence:** Define the scope of this thread ("A foundational line of work approaches X through...")
2. **Key works:** Compact narrative summary, not a citation list
3. **Limitation:** Connected to the technical challenge YOUR method solves
4. **Bridge:** Transition that leads toward your contribution

## Style Rules

**Dialogue feel, not roll call:**
- Yes: "A pioneering line of work by [X] established the paradigm of..."
- Yes: "Building on [Y]'s insight that Z, subsequent work explored..."
- No: "[X] et al. [1] proposed method A. [Y] et al. [2] proposed method B."

**Honest positioning:**
- Cover the strongest and most recent competitors — don't hide them
- Compare mechanisms and failure modes, not just accuracy numbers
- State your difference in technical terms, not marketing terms

## Quality Checklist

1. Are all strong/recent competitors covered?
2. Is each thread connected to your problem setting?
3. Does each paragraph end with a clear relationship to your work?
4. Is your novelty easy to verify from this section alone?
