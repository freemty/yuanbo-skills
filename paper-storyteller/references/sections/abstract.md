# Abstract Guide

## Narrative Goal

The movie trailer of the paper — a complete story arc in 150-250 words.

## Writing Rhythm

```
Sentence 1    Hook — the problem or phenomenon (compressed from intro ¶1)
Sentence 2    Gap — what current methods can't do and why
Sentences 3-5 Method — your approach with the core metaphor, key mechanism in 2-3 sentences
Sentences 6-7 Results — strongest numbers + what they prove
```

## Key Rules

1. The core metaphor in the abstract MUST match the intro. No new metaphors here.
2. No concepts should appear in the abstract that aren't in the intro.
3. Write the abstract LAST — after intro, method, and experiments are solid.
4. Every sentence carries one message. If a sentence mixes two ideas, split it.

## Template Variants

### Variant A: Challenge → Contribution
1. Task. 2. Technical challenge. 3. One sentence naming the contribution. 4. Benefits. 5. Results.

### Variant B: Challenge → Insight → Contribution
1. Task. 2. Technical challenge. 3. One sentence stating the insight. 4. One sentence naming the contribution that implements the insight. 5. Benefits. 6. Results.

### Variant C: Multiple Contributions
1. Task. 2. Brief prior art contrast. 3. Contribution 1 + advantage. 4. Contribution 2 + advantage. 5. Results.

## Quality Checklist

1. Can a reader identify task, challenge, insight, contribution, and results in one pass?
2. Does the hook sentence create curiosity (not just state the task)?
3. Is every claim supported by the results sentences?
4. Are technical names self-contained and readable without intro context?
